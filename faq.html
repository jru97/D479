Frequently Asked Questions
Power outlets are the same as in the United States, 120 volts.
The drinking age on Taniti is 18 years and older. Alcohol is not served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9 am.
Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English, however older or more rural residents may speak little to no English.
Taniti has one hospital, and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees and translators are available as needed.
Violent crime is extremely rare. As tourism increases, petty crimes like pickpocketing are increasing. If you are the victim of a crime, please call 111 for the police.
Taniti celebrates a large number of national holidays, please check the official calendar to see if any coincide with your visit. Please note that most restaurants and attractions will be closed on holidays.
Taniti uses the US dollar as its official currency, however many business also accept European euros and Japanese yen. All banks on the island can facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses also accept major credit cards.
